Encyclopedia

In the course of a typical simulation in the Game of Life, a variety of distinct patterns emerge which are essential to the unfolding dynamics of the environment. These patterns can be broadly categorized based on their behaviors into static patterns, oscillating patterns, and moving patterns.

  • Static Patterns

    These are formations that remain stationary, retaining their structure over successive generations without undergoing any change. They represent a stable state in the simulation.

  • Oscillating Patterns

    Oscillating patterns are characterized by a repetitive cycle of transformations, wherein they fluctuate between two or more states in a regular pattern. These oscillations create a dynamic yet predictable series of formations as time progresses.

  • Moving Patterns

    Also known as "spaceships," these patterns exhibit locomotion across the grid, altering their position over sequential generations. They traverse the environment, potentially interacting with other entities on the grid.

  • Generative Patterns

    In addition to the above categories, there are generative patterns that spawn other formations, leading to rich and complex evolving dynamics. They may give birth to a range of other patterns, encompassing static, oscillating, or moving types, thereby introducing new elements into the simulation.

The upcoming sections provide an illustrative guide to the most frequently encountered patterns in the Game of Life, offering insights into their characteristics and the roles they play in the larger simulation ecology."

Static Patterns

Block

A 2x2 grid of alive cells

Beehive

A formation of six cells that resembles a beehive

Loaf

A configuration of seven cells that somewhat looks like a loaf of bread

Boat

A pattern of five cells that forms a shape reminiscent of a boat

Tub

A smaller formation of four cells creating a tub-like shape

Pond

A configuration of eight cells that outline the shape of a pond

Oscillating Patterns

Blinker

A line of three cells that alternates between a vertical and horizontal orientation

Toad

A formation of six cells that alternates between two different states

Beacon

A pattern that oscillates between a 2x2 block and two separated blocks

Pulsar

A larger oscillator that has a period of 3 and forms a distinctive star-like pattern

Pentadecathlon

An oscillator with a period of 15, having a quite long and narrow shape

Clock

A smaller oscillator with a period of 2, shifting between two configurations resembling a clock's hands

Moving Patterns

Glider

A small pattern that moves diagonally across the grid, one step every four generations

Lightweight Spaceship

A pattern that moves either horizontally or vertically across the grid over the span of four generations

Middleweight Spaceship

Similar to the LWSS but slightly larger, traversing the grid over four generations

Heavyweight Spaceship

A larger version of the MWSS, moving in a steady pattern over four generations

Loafer

A spaceship that drifts slowly across the grid in a straight line

Crawler

A category of spaceships that move slowly, often diagonally, across the grid

Generative Patterns

Glider Gun

A configuration that continuously generates gliders, producing a stream of them

Gosper's puffer train

A moving pattern that leaves a trail of static "smoke" patterns as it moves

R-pentomino

A pattern that, despite its initial small size, evolves into a chaotic and long-lasting configuration, eventually stabilizing with a collection of static patterns and oscillators

Diehard

A pattern that takes 130 generations to stabilize, eventually disappearing completely, leaving no cells behind

Acorn

A small pattern that exhibits a chaotic growth, eventually stabilizing into a combination of still lifes, oscillators, and spaceships

Bunnies

A pattern that generates a chaotic growth, stabilizing after several generations into a mixture of still lifes and oscillators
